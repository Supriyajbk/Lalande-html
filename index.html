Personal Injury Law: Car Accidents

The most serious car accidents often result from head-on collisions, rollovers, or high-speed collisions. Rollover car accidents are particularly dangerous, as they can lead to occupants being ejected from the vehicle, especially if seatbelts are not used. Head-on collisions, while less common, are extremely severe due to the combined force of two vehicles moving towards each other, often resulting in fatal or life-threatening injuries. High-speed car accidents, typically occurring on highways or freeways, can cause catastrophic damage due to the immense force involved, often resulting in multi-car pileups.

In our experience as Hamilton personal injury lawyers, the most serious injuries from car accidents include:

  • traumatic brain injuries (TBI)
  • spinal cord injuries, and
  • amputations

Traumatic brain injuries can range from concussions to severe brain damage, affecting a person’s cognitive and physical abilities, sometimes permanently.

Spinal cord injuries can result in paralysis, affecting a person’s mobility and independence. Amputations, whether immediate or as a result of surgical intervention due to severe injury, can dramatically alter a person’s life, requiring extensive rehabilitation and adaptation to new ways of living. These types of injuries not only have a significant physical and emotional impact but also result in substantial financial burdens due to medical expenses, rehabilitation costs, and potential loss of income.

Some results we achieved for our car accident clients include:

  • $3M for a spinal cord injury victim (combined tort and AB)
  • $2.8M for an amputee victim
  • $2.0M for a young amputee victim
  • $1.3M for a man with a spinal cord injury from an impaired driver
  • $980,000 for a woman with a serious brain injury
  • $2.0M for a woman with a traumatic brain injury
  • $1.1M for a spinal cord injury victim
  • $1.3M for a middle age medical professional
  • $710,000 for an amputee victim

While these are never taken to be guarantees of future case results, they demonstrate some of the successes of Hamilton Personal Injury Lawyer Matt Lalande for his clients following crashes and injuries.

  • spinal cord injuries, and
  • amputations

Traumatic brain injuries can range from concussions to severe brain damage, affecting a person’s cognitive and physical abilities, sometimes permanently.

Spinal cord injuries can result in paralysis, affecting a person’s mobility and independence. Amputations, whether immediate or as a result of surgical intervention due to severe injury, can dramatically alter a person’s life, requiring extensive rehabilitation and adaptation to new ways of living. These types of injuries not only have a significant physical and emotional impact but also result in substantial financial burdens due to medical expenses, rehabilitation costs, and potential loss of income.
